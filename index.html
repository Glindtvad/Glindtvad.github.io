Who am I?






Hello and welcome to my personal website.
My name is Sebastian and I hail from the small country of Denmark. I was born in 1990 in the city of Aarhus (Smilets By) where I spend my next 15 years continually, yet this streak would not last. In 2005, my family and I travelled to Australia and lived in the small town of Engadine in Sutherland Shire, just south of Sydney. I attended Engadine Highschool for the next half a year, and spent every weekend exploring the surrounding area and more distant locations such as Cairns in the holidays with my family. After a wonderful half year of adventures, we returned to Denmark, where I attended a Danish high school (Aarhus Tech, Viby) which focus heavily on natural science subjects. I took my undergraduate in general biology at the University of Aarhus, with my last project entailing the effect of protein rich and sugar rich diets on Australian weaver ant workers. During my undergraduate education, I took time off and travelled to Fiji and Thailand as a volunteer. For four months, I helped with underwater surveys, mangrove restoration and helping local communities. After finishing my undergraduate, I took my Master of Science in Biology at University of Aarhus, with half a year spent abroad in Norway, and another 8 months in Greenland as part of courses and my master’s thesis. Since my graduation, I have worked as a research assistant to my old supervisor, been an intern at NAFO (Page 17) in Halifax , Canada, been a highschool teacher at my old highschool in Denmark and currently working at the Institute for Marine Research in Bergen, Norway.
